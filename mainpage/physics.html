SCT Physics

The physics program of the SCT experiment is discussed in detail in CDR, vol. 1. It can be roughly divied into three parts:

  1. Physics of the charm quark
  2. Physics of the tau lepton
  3. Precision measuerment of QCD

The cards below show the main topics of the SCT physics program.

Charm

  • Measurement of the strong phases of \(D^0\) decay amplitudes
  • Measurement of the absolute branching fractions
  • Searches for rare and forbidden decays of the charm quark
  • Measurement of the CP symmetry breaking in charm decay and production

Tau

  • Precision measurement of the \(\tau\) lepton properties
  • Measurement of the Michel parameters, tests of the lepton universality
  • Precision measurement of hadronic \(\tau\) decays
  • Search for CP symmetry breaking in \(\tau\) production and decays

QCD

  • Physics of highly-exited quarkonium
  • Hadronic molecular states
  • Baryon interaction at threshold
  • Search for glueballs in decays of \(J/\psi\) and \(\phi(2S)\)
